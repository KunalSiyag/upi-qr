For non-profit organizations, temple trusts, and community organizers, collecting donations efficiently and transparently is critical. Traditionally, donations were collected via cash boxes or bank transfers requiring complex account numbers and IFSC codes.

Today, Unified Payments Interface (UPI) has changed the landscape. With a simple scan, donors can transfer funds instantly from their bank account to yours. Best of all, standard UPI-to-UPI transactions are **completely commission-free**, ensuring 100% of the donation goes directly to your cause.

![A donation box with a custom printed Scan to Pay card](/images/blog/donation-box-guide.png)

In this guide, we will cover how to set up, print, and securely display a UPI QR code for donation collection.

---

## The Benefits of UPI for Charitable Donations

Using UPI for fundraising offers several advantages over cash and credit cards:

1. **Zero Platform Fees:** Standard payment gateways charge a transaction fee of 2% to 3%. UPI transactions to a direct bank VPA have **0% transaction fees**.
2. **Instant Settlement:** Funds are credited directly to your bank account in real-time, rather than being held by a third-party gateway for 2–3 days.
3. **Frictionless Donating:** Donors don't need to carry cash or type long bank account numbers. They scan, enter the amount, and pay in 5 seconds.
4. **Improved Transparency:** Donors see the official name of the trust or charity on their screens before confirming the payment, building trust.

## Step 1: Set Up a Trust or Business Bank Account

For legal, regulatory, and tax compliance:

* **Do not use a personal UPI ID** for public donations.
* Set up a dedicated current/savings account in the name of the Temple Trust, NGO, or Organization.
* Contact your bank to request a **Merchant UPI ID** (VPA). This will look like `templetrust@sbi` or `ngohelp@icici`.
* Having a merchant VPA ensures you can receive large volumes of transactions without hitting personal daily transaction limits (which are usually capped at ₹1 Lakh per day for personal accounts).

## Step 3: Print and Place the Poster Effectively

Where you position the QR code card makes a major difference in collection rates:

* **Donation Desks and Counters:** Place an **A5 (5" x 7") or A4 (8" x 11") standee** on a sturdy acrylic stand at reception counters, registration tables, or next to cash collection boxes.
* **Banners and Notice Boards:** If you are running an event, embed the QR code on a larger welcome banner or notice board. Ensure the QR code itself is at least **6 inches wide** so it can be scanned easily from 4–6 feet away.
* **Digital Sharing:** Share the generated card directly in newsletter updates, WhatsApp groups, or on your organization's website.

---

## Step 4: Add Donator Verification Instructions

To prevent confusion and ensure bookkeeping is clean, display a small instruction note next to the QR code standee:

> *"Please mention your Name and Purpose in the UPI payment note/remarks field before sending. Send a screenshot of the confirmation to our WhatsApp number (+91 XXXXX XXXXX) if you require a formal donation receipt."*

This is particularly useful if your NGO provides tax exemptions under Section 80G, as you will need the donor's PAN and address details to issue tax certificates.

## Summary Checklists for Donation QR Codes

* **Verify the Payee Name:** Scan the QR code using [Google Pay](/google-pay-qr-generator/), [PhonePe](/phonepe-qr-generator/), and [Paytm](/paytm-qr-generator/). Check that the verified merchant name matches your trust/NGO name exactly.
* **Test the Limits:** Verify with your bank if there are monthly inbound caps on your merchant account.
* **Maintain Security:** Keep your UPI PIN safe. Remember: **You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
