Displaying the wrong QR — or a tampered one — means lost revenue and angry customers. Banks and NPCI recommend merchants verify payment credentials before every standee goes live.

Run this checklist once per QR print, and again monthly on physical counters.

---

## Checklist 1: Digital Verification (Before Printing)

1. **Match payee name:** The name in your generator must match the bank-verified name shown in GPay/PhonePe when someone pays you.
2. **Confirm VPA spelling:** One wrong character in `pa=yourshop@okaxis` routes money elsewhere.
3. **Scan the digital preview:** Use a second phone to scan the on-screen QR before downloading.
4. **Optional ₹1 test:** Send ₹1 from a family member's UPI app and confirm it hits your bank statement.

Generate and preview on the [Pro UPI QR Generator](/).

---

## Checklist 2: Physical Verification (After Printing)

1. Scan the **printed** card, not just the screen file.
2. Check payee name at **1 meter** and **2 meters** — lighting at the counter matters.
3. Inspect for **sticker tampering** — scratch tests on corners to detect overlay fraud.
4. Sign or mark the back of acrylic holders so replacements are obvious.

Read [prevent UPI QR tampering and frauds](/blog/prevent-upi-qr-code-tampering-frauds/).

---

## Checklist 3: Staff Handover

* Tell staff the **correct payee name** customers should see.
* If name shows a personal name instead of shop name, consider upgrading to a [merchant VPA](/blog/understanding-upi-vpa-merchant-accounts/).
* Never ask customers to scan a QR to **receive** refunds — that is a scam pattern. See [is it safe to scan UPI QR](/blog/is-it-safe-to-scan-upi-qr-code/).

---

## Red Flags — Do Not Display

| Red flag | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Payee name mismatch | Regenerate QR with correct VPA from bank app |
| QR scans to a website, not UPI app | Remove immediately — possible phishing |
| Unknown sticker over your QR | Peel, replace, report if needed |
| Blurry or faded print | Reprint at 300 DPI matte |
