The integration of RuPay Credit Cards with UPI has opened up credit payments for millions of consumers in India. However, unlike standard bank-to-bank UPI transfers which are completely free (0% MDR), credit card UPI transactions are subject to processing fees.

In this guide, we break down what charges apply to merchants when customers scan their QR codes using a linked RuPay credit card.

---

## The Rule: Small Merchants are Exempt

To protect small micro-merchants and kirana stores, the NPCI has set clear rules regarding MDR:
* **Transactions under ₹2,000:** There is **zero MDR (0% charge)** for transactions under ₹2,000 processed on RuPay credit cards via UPI.
* **Small Merchants:** Small retail outlets with low annual turnovers are exempt from credit card processing fees for standard micro-payments.

---

## Charges for Large Merchants (Transactions over ₹2,000)

For transactions exceeding **₹2,000** processed on RuPay credit cards:
* An MDR of up to **2.0% + GST** (typically shared between the card issuing bank and the UPI app provider) may be charged to the merchant.
* This is similar to the charges applied when swiping a physical credit card on a Point of Sale (POS) machine.

---

## How Merchants Can Manage Credit Card UPI

* **Reconciliation:** Check your business app (GPay Business/PhonePe Business) settings. You can choose to disable RuPay Credit Card acceptance if you only want to accept 100% free bank-to-bank UPI transfers.
* **Keep Track:** Most daily customer payments at local stores are under ₹2,000, meaning you will rarely pay any transaction fees while still offering convenient credit payment options to your customers.
