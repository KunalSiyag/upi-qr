Clinic reception queues slow down when staff handle cash and change. A verified **UPI QR at the billing desk** lets patients scan, pay consultation fees, and receive digital receipts on their phone — while the clinic's bank account credits instantly.

---

## Where to Place UPI QR in a Clinic

| Location | Payment type | QR type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| OPD reception | Consultation fee | Dynamic (fixed consult fee) or static |
| Diagnostics desk | Test package | Dynamic per package |
| Pharmacy window | Medicine bill | Usually static (variable totals) |
| Parking / canteen | Fixed charges | Dynamic amount |

Use separate VPAs or clear transaction notes (`OPD`, `LAB-102`) if accounting departments differ.

---

## Setup Steps

1. Open a **current account** in the clinic or hospital trust name (avoid personal doctor UPI for institutional billing).
2. Request **merchant VPA** from the bank — e.g., `cityclinic@sbi`.
3. Generate standee on [Pro UPI QR](/) with registered trust/clinic name as payee.
4. Print A5 counter standee, [verify payee name](/blog/how-to-verify-upi-qr-code-before-displaying/) with ₹1 test.
5. Train reception to confirm payment screenshot before token issue.

---

## Compliance and Patient Trust

* Display **clinic registered name** exactly as bank-verified on UPI screen.
* Issue printed or SMS receipt with UPI reference number (UTR).
* Do not collect advance via QR without appointment confirmation policy.
* For high-value surgeries, combine UPI with formal invoice — QR alone is not a legal receipt substitute without your billing system.

NPCI UPI handles payment rail only; maintain your clinical records and GST compliance separately.

---

## Reducing Queue Time

* Pre-print **dynamic QRs** for standard consult fees (₹500 general, ₹800 specialist).
* Add QR to appointment SMS/WhatsApp — see [WhatsApp payment links](/blog/how-to-create-whatsapp-payment-link-upi/).
* Optional soundbox for "payment received" audio at busy desks.
