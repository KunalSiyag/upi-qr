Freelancers in India often lose 2–3% to payment gateways on every client invoice. If your client pays via direct **UPI bank transfer**, you keep 100% of the fee (subject to your tax obligations).

A UPI QR on your invoice replaces "please NEFT to account X" with one scan-and-pay action.

---

## Why Freelancers Use UPI QR

* **Zero MDR** on standard UPI-to-bank payments.
* **Instant settlement** — no 2-day gateway hold.
* **Works on WhatsApp** — send PNG invoice card directly.
* **Professional look** — branded Freelancer Payment Card template on [Pro UPI QR](/).

---

## Step-by-Step: QR on a ₹25,000 Invoice

1. Create invoice in Excel, Google Docs, or billing app with line items and total.
2. Open [Pro UPI QR Generator](/).
3. Enter your **registered name** (or trade name clients recognize).
4. Enter UPI ID — prefer a dedicated business VPA if registered.
5. In **Amount**, enter `25000` for this specific invoice (dynamic QR).
6. In **Note**, enter `INV-2026-042` so you can reconcile.
7. Download Freelancer Payment Card PNG and attach below invoice total.

For recurring variable amounts, leave amount blank (static QR) and type the figure in the invoice text.

## Best Practices

| Practice | Why |
| :--- | :--- |
| Unique note per invoice (`INV-042`) | Easier bank statement matching |
| Send QR + UPI ID text | Backup if image fails |
| Mention GST line separately | QR amount is total payable |
| Use professional name | Builds trust vs personal nickname VPA |

## When to Use a Payment Gateway Instead

Use Razorpay/Stripe if you need:
* International cards in foreign currency
* Automated GST invoices with payment reconciliation
* Refund APIs and chargeback workflow
* Website checkout embed
