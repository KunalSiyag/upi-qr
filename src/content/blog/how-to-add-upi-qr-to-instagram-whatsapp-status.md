Home businesses and Instagram sellers lose sales when customers must DM for payment details. Posting your **UPI QR image** on WhatsApp Status, Instagram Stories, or a pinned catalog message cuts friction to one scan.

---

## WhatsApp Business (Best Channel in India)

### Method 1: Status Image
1. Generate clean QR card on [Pro UPI QR](/).
2. Open WhatsApp Business → **Status** → upload PNG.
3. Caption: "Scan to pay | [Shop Name] | Mention order ID in note."

### Method 2: Catalog / Quick Reply
1. Save QR PNG to phone.
2. Create **Quick Reply** shortcut `/pay` with image attached.
3. Send instantly when customer confirms order.

### Method 3: Payment Link Text
Copy `upi://pay?...` link from generator and send as tappable link — detailed in [WhatsApp payment link guide](/blog/how-to-create-whatsapp-payment-link-upi/).

---

## Instagram

* **Stories:** Post QR with "Scan to order" sticker; pin best story to Highlights named "Pay".
* **Bio link:** Instagram allows one link — use your website or Linktree with UPI QR image page. Direct `upi://` links may not be clickable in bio — image scan is more reliable.
* **DM sales:** Send QR PNG after order confirmation (same as WhatsApp).

---

## Facebook Marketplace & YouTube Community

* Upload QR as comment image on marketplace listings.
* Pin payment QR in Facebook group announcements for local sellers.
* YouTube Community tab image post for creators selling merch/courses.

---

## Design Tips for Social

| Tip | Reason |
| :--- | :--- |
| Use branded template, not raw QR | Builds trust on social |
| Include shop name + UPI ID text below QR | Backup if image compresses |
| Avoid heavy Instagram filters | Filters break QR modules |
| Re-post monthly | Status expires; keeps visibility |

Use [custom GPay & PhonePe templates](/blog/custom-design-templates-gpay-phonepe-qr/) for consistent branding.

---

## Security on Social

* Public QR only **receives** money — safe to post for collections.
* Never post screenshots asking customers to scan to **get refunds**.
* Watch for scammers impersonating your shop — use consistent payee name.
