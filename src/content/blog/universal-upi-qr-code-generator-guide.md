With dozens of UPI payment apps active in India—such as PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, BHIM, and bank-specific apps—displaying a separate QR code standee for each app creates counter clutter and customer confusion.

The solution is a **Universal UPI QR Code**. A single universal QR code can accept payments from any UPI app, redirecting the customer seamlessly to their preferred application.

## What is a Universal UPI QR Code?

A universal UPI QR code is a standardized graphic that encodes a standard **UPI Unified Resource Identifier (URI)**. Instead of using proprietary app-specific deep links, it uses the standardized schema defined by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI).

The structure of the data inside a universal QR code looks like this:
`upi://pay?pa=merchant@upi&pn=BusinessName&cu=INR`

When a customer scans this QR, their smartphone's operating system checks which apps are registered to handle the `upi://` protocol. It then displays a menu of available payment apps (or automatically launches their default one) with the merchant's details pre-filled.

## Comparison: Static vs. Dynamic Universal QR Codes

Before generating your QR code, you must decide between a static and dynamic format.

| Feature | Static Universal QR | Dynamic Universal QR |
|---|---|---|
| **Amount Field** | Optional (Customer enters amount manually) | Fixed (Locked amount, customer cannot edit) |
| **Note/Reference** | Static (Same note for all scans) | Unique (Different order ID or customer ID per scan) |
| **Use Case** | Checkout counters, donation tables, flyers | Invoices, payment gateways, checkout screens |
| **Internet Dependency** | No (Generates once, works offline forever) | Yes (Requires API generation for each transaction) |
| **Cost** | 100% Free | Often requires paid API subscription |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Do universal UPI QR codes expire?
No. Standard static UPI QR codes generated by our tool are permanent and never expire. They contain no middleman redirections, meaning they link directly to your VPA. They will continue to work as long as your linked bank account remains active.

### Q2. Is there a transaction limit for universal UPI QR codes?
For personal UPI accounts, the standard limit set by NPCI is ₹1 Lakh per day. For verified merchant UPI accounts (such as GST-registered VPAs), the limit can extend up to ₹5 Lakhs per day depending on the merchant category.

### Q3. Are there any processing fees or commissions?
No. Peer-to-peer (P2P) transfers via standard UPI QR codes are 100% free under NPCI guidelines. You receive the exact amount sent by the customer directly into your bank account with zero MDR (Merchant Discount Rate) deductions.
